Impacts to Manufacturing

PRODUCTIVITY

One of the most recognized benefits of implementing manufacturing robots is the improvment in productivity and efficiency.. In most cases, robots can perform tasks at greater speeds than humans are able to and are not restrained by fatigue or the need to take breaks. Movements for repetitive tasks are very precise, and time is not lost for unnecessary travel. This precision also improves the quality of the final product which leads to costs savings due to less scrap materials.

SAFETY

Introducing robotics into certain tasks and environments can reduce worker exposure to hazards. For instance, robots can replace a person when production requires the use of hazardous materials or exposure to extreme temperatures. Similarly, jobs that are very repetitive or have poor ergonomics can be replaced by robots to reduce the physical impact on employees.

QUALITY

Robotics enhances product quality through precision and consistency. Their accurate, repeatable movements eliminate human error, which is especially important when assembling small or intricate products such as electronics or medical devices. This precision leads to fewer defects, less scrap material, and more reliable final products.

COST

Robots have a high initial cost that extends beyond the cost of the actual robots. Engineering and installation costs during implementation along with the cost of programming and startup typically exceed the cost of the robot. There may also be costs associated with an increase in scrap products during the testing phase. Once the robot is installed and turned over to production, ongoing preventative maintenance costs for labor, programming updates, and spare parts inventory must also be considered.

DOWNTIME AND RELIABILITY

Working around robots can be dangerous if the proper safety precautions are not put in place or if they are bypassed. This could lead to workplace injuries, damage to other equipment, scrap products, and production downtime. With the elevated level of complexity found in the construction of manufacturing robots including moving parts, sensors, actuators, and processors, there are several points of failure which could lead to extended downtime and loss of revenue in the event of a component failure.

TECHNICAL SKILLS

Hiring or outsourcing the technical expertise required for maintaining or upgrading a robot can also be challenging, especially for rural manufacturers.
