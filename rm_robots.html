Types of Manufacturing Robots

Cartesian

Cartesian robots, also known as linear robots, operate on three linear axes (X, Y, Z). Their simple design allows for precise movement, making them ideal for tasks such as pick-and-place operations in assembly lines.

Cylindrical

Cylindrical robots utilize a cylindrical coordinate system, offering a larger workspace than Cartesian robots. They are commonly used in applications requiring vertical movement, such as assembly and packaging.

SCARA

Selective Compliance Assembly Robot Arm (SCARA) robots are designed for horizontal movements, making them suitable for assembly tasks. Their rigidity and flexibility enhance speed and precision, particularly in electronics manufacturing.

Delta

Delta robots are characterized by their parallel arms, allowing for high-speed operations. They excel in packaging and sorting applications due to their rapid movement and ability to handle lightweight products efficiently.

Articulated

Articulated robots feature multiple rotary joints, providing a high degree of flexibility and range of motion. This versatility makes them ideal for complex tasks such as welding, painting, and assembling intricate components.

Cobots

Collaborative robots, also known as cobots, are designed to work alongside humans, enhancing productivity through cooperative work. Unlike traditional industrial robots, cobots are equipped with safety features that allow them to operate safely in proximity to human workers. Their adaptability makes them particularly beneficial in small and medium-sized enterprises, where they can assist with repetitive tasks without requiring extensive safety measures.
